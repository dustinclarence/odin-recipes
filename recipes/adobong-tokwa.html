Adobong Tokwa

Description

A savory Filipino tofu dish made with crispy fried tofu simmered in a rich adobo sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, onions, and black pepper. Each tofu cube is coated in a glossy, flavorful sauce and finished with fresh spring onions and crispy garlic for added aroma and texture.

This dish is easy to prepare, budget-friendly, and pairs perfectly with steamed rice. It is a favorite among vegetarians and anyone looking for a simple yet satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 500 g firm tofu (tokwa), cut into cubes
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 tbsp chopped spring onions
  • Extra fried garlic for topping (optional)

Steps

  1. Pat the tofu dry and cut it into cubes.
  2. Heat the cooking oil in a pan over medium heat.
  3. Fry the tofu until all sides are golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  4. Using the same pan, sauté the minced garlic until lightly golden.
  5. Add the diced onion and cook until softened.
  6. Pour in the soy sauce and vinegar. Let it simmer for about 1 minute without stirring.
  7. Add the water, brown sugar, and black pepper. Simmer for 3–5 minutes.
  8. Add the fried tofu and gently toss until evenly coated with the sauce.
  9. Cook for another 2–3 minutes to allow the tofu to absorb the flavors.
  10. Transfer to a serving plate and garnish with chopped spring onions and fried garlic before serving.
