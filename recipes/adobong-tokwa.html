Adobong Tokwa

Description

Adobong Tokwa is a delicious Filipino tofu dish made with crispy fried tofu simmered in a savory adobo sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, onions, brown sugar, and black pepper. The tofu absorbs the rich flavors of the sauce while remaining slightly crisp on the outside.

This budget-friendly and easy-to-make dish is perfect for vegetarians or anyone looking for a simple yet satisfying meal. It is best served with steamed rice and topped with fresh spring onions and crispy fried garlic.

Ingredients

  • 500 g firm tofu (tokwa)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)
  • 2 tbsp chopped spring onions
  • 2 tbsp fried garlic (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Pat the tofu dry with paper towels and cut it into evenly sized cubes.
  2. Heat the cooking oil in a pan over medium heat.
  3. Fry the tofu until all sides are golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  4. Using the same pan, sauté the minced garlic until lightly golden.
  5. Add the diced onion and cook until softened.
  6. Pour in the soy sauce and vinegar. Let the mixture simmer for about 1 minute without stirring.
  7. Add the water, brown sugar, ground black pepper, and bay leaf (if using).
  8. Simmer for 3–5 minutes.
  9. Add the fried tofu and gently toss until evenly coated with the sauce.
  10. Cook for another 2–3 minutes to allow the tofu to absorb the flavors.
  11. Transfer to a serving plate and garnish with chopped spring onions and fried garlic before serving.
